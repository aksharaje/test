# Research Planner

## Overview
The Research Planner tool helps Product Managers streamline the planning and execution of research activities, enabling them to make data-driven decisions and gather valuable insights for product development.

## Key Capabilities
- Create new research planning sessions
- View and manage existing research sessions
- Select research methods for investigation
- Update interview guides and survey questions
- Generate instruments for selected research methods

## How to Use
To begin, start by creating a new research planning session where you define the objective and context for your research. Then, you can select specific research methods to proceed with, update interview guides and survey questions as needed, and generate instruments for the selected methods.

## Configuration & Fields
- **Objective**: Describes the main goal or purpose of the research session.
  - **What it's for**: Helps you focus on the key outcomes you want to achieve.
  - **Example**: "Improve user onboarding experience for our mobile app."

- **Research Context**: Provides additional context or background information for the research.
  - **What it's for**: Helps team members understand the research scope and constraints.
  - **Example**: "Current user feedback suggests issues with the checkout process."

- **User ID**: Identifies the user associated with the research session (optional).
  - **What it's for**: Links the session to a specific team member for collaboration.
  - **Example**: "Assign this session to the UX designer, user_id=123."

- **Connected Knowledge**: Lists relevant knowledge base IDs for reference (optional).
  - **What it's for**: Links the session to existing knowledge resources for deeper insights.
  - **Example**: "Refer to previous customer survey results, knowledge_base_ids=[456, 789]."
