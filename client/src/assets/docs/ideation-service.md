# Ideation Manager

## Overview
Ideation Manager helps Product Managers facilitate ideation sessions and achieve SMART goals effectively. It streamlines the process of generating and organizing innovative ideas to drive product development and strategic planning.

## Key Capabilities
- Create new ideation sessions
- Retrieve session details
- List sessions with pagination
- Retry failed sessions
- Update idea fields
- Delete sessions and related data
- Run the ideation pipeline for idea generation and evaluation

## How to Use
To start, create a new ideation session by defining the problem statement, constraints, goals, and research insights. Then, collaborate with team members to generate and prioritize ideas. Review and update idea details as needed. Finally, run the ideation pipeline to evaluate and refine the generated ideas.

## Configuration & Fields
- **Problem Statement**: Describes the main challenge or opportunity.
  - **What it's for**: Clearly articulates the focus of the ideation session.
  - **Example**: "Improve user engagement on the mobile app."
  
- **Constraints**: Limits or conditions that need to be considered during ideation.
  - **What it's for**: Sets boundaries for idea generation.
  - **Example**: "Budget constraints limit new feature development."

- **Goals**: Desired outcomes or objectives to achieve through ideation.
  - **What it's for**: Defines the success criteria for the session.
  - **Example**: "Increase customer retention by 15%."

- **Research Insights**: Relevant information or data to inform the ideation process.
  - **What it's for**: Provides background context for idea generation.
  - **Example**: "User feedback indicates a need for improved navigation."

- **Connected Knowledge**: IDs of knowledge bases or resources linked to the ideation session.
  - **What it's for**: Integrates external information sources into the ideation process.
  - **Example**: "Linked customer survey results and market research reports."
